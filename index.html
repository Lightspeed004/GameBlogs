Recommendation of the Week

Let's talk about the newest DLC from the infamous company known for introducing the souls genre to the world, FromSoftware, and let me tell you, they've outdone themselves. Shadow of the Erdtree for Elden Ring isn't just good, it's a masterclass in expansion design. Imagine everything you loved about Elden Ring – the brutal combat, the cryptic lore begging to be unraveled, the breathtaking world design – condensed into a perfectly formed, incredibly tight package. Shadow of the Erdtree feels like a whole new game in itself, boasting a vast new map brimming with secrets to uncover. We're talking a staggering amount of new content: 70 weapons to add to your arsenal, a staggering number of spells and incantations to master, and enough armor sets to outfit a small army. FromSoftware hasn't just increased the quantity though, the quality is phenomenal. Each new addition feels unique and exciting to use, perfectly complementing the established gameplay of Elden Ring. The average player would spend about 50 hours before the credits start rolling, this is actually longer than many full priced games, although this DLC isn't particularly cheap either. FromSoft has once again delivered a masterpiece
